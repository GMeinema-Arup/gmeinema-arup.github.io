This multidisciplinary research project investigates how Ricanau Mofo, a low-lying village in Suriname, can become more water-adaptive and sustainable while facing increasing challenges from land erosion, riverbank erosion, changing rainfall patterns and sea level rise. The study evaluates interventions that balance technical effectiveness with cultural preservation, accessibility, affordability and ease of maintenance. 【1-59f505】

Three complementary strategies were developed. The first focuses on mitigating land erosion through vegetation planting and, in the longer term, footpaths combined with drainage channels. The second addresses riverbank erosion using vegetated wooden bulkheads as a short-term solution and a groyne-based riverbank protection system as a long-term measure. The third strategy focuses on reducing water damage in the built environment through building guidance, educational information boards and a flood early warning system. 【1-59f505】

The project extends beyond conventional reporting by delivering prototype interventions, educational materials in the local language and a business case to support applications for future funding. The proposed measures were designed to be scalable to other villages along the Cottica River and elsewhere in Suriname. 【1-59f505】

The research highlights the importance of combining engineering, environmental and social considerations in climate adaptation projects. While data availability limited the precision of some analyses, the study demonstrates practical pathways for reducing water-related risks and improving community resilience in vulnerable rural environments. 【1-59f505】
