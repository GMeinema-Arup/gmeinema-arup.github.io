This MSc thesis develops a decision-making framework to evaluate strengthening interventions for prestressed concrete bridge girders against full replacement. The framework combines structural reliability analysis, service-life prediction, environmental and economic assessment, multi-objective optimisation, and multi-criteria decision-making. 【1-ef770e】

The research compares four strengthening techniques—external prestressing (EP), memory steel (MS), carbon fibre reinforced polymer (CFRP), and ultra-high-performance concrete (UHPC)—with complete bridge replacement. The methodology was applied primarily to shear-critical precast prestressed T-beam bridges. 【1-ef770e】

A parametric study demonstrated that CFRP was most frequently selected as the preferred strengthening option due to its favourable strength-to-weight ratio, resulting in lower material consumption and environmental impact. External prestressing proved competitive for longer spans, while memory steel generally performed less favourably within the considered cases. 【1-ef770e】

The developed framework is intended as a decision-support tool for bridge owners and engineers facing future replacement and renovation challenges associated with ageing infrastructure. 【1-ef770e】
